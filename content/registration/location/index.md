## Host City: Ohrid, North Macedonia {class=text-center}
{{< columns >}}

<p>Ohrid is one of the most distinguished cultural and historical cities in Southeast Europe, uniquely combining rich heritage with exceptional natural beauty. Situated on the shores of Lake Ohrid, one of the oldest and deepest lakes in Europe, the city represents a rare harmony of history, spirituality, and landscape.
</p>
<p>
Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, both the city of Ohrid and Lake Ohrid are celebrated for their outstanding universal value. The old town is characterized by narrow cobblestone streets, traditional architecture, and a remarkable concentration of early Christian churches, monasteries, and archaeological remains spanning several centuries. This unique setting has made Ohrid a long-standing center of education, culture, and intellectual exchange in the region.
</p>
<p>
In addition to its historical significance, Ohrid is a vibrant and welcoming city, known for its hospitality, traditional Macedonian cuisine, and relaxed atmosphere. The lakeside promenade, cultural landmarks, and panoramic views provide an inspiring environment for professional meetings and international gatherings.<br/>
</p>
